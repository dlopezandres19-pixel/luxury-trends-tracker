📊 How to Read This Dashboard
What is Google Trends Search Interest?
Google Trends measures relative search popularity on a scale of 0-100, where 100 represents peak search interest for the selected time period and region. These are NOT absolute search volumes, but rather normalized scores that show the relative popularity of search terms.
Data Source: Search queries on Google from mainland China (geo='CN') and worldwide. Updated automatically daily at 3:00 AM UTC.
Understanding the Numbers
  • Search Interest Score (0-100): Higher numbers = more search activity. A score of 68 means searches are at 68% of the peak level for that period.
  • ↑ Arrow Up (+%): Search interest INCREASED compared to the previous period (7 days ago, 30 days ago, or start of 12-month period).
  • ↓ Arrow Down (-%): Search interest DECREASED compared to the previous period.
  • → Flat (0%): No significant change in search interest.
Example Interpretation:
Louis Vuitton: 68 ↑ +19.3%
Current search interest is at 68/100 (high). Searches increased by 19.3% compared to the start of the period. This suggests growing consumer interest in the brand.
What Does This Measure & Why It Matters
Proxy for Consumer Interest: Search volume is a leading indicator of purchase intent. When more people search for "Louis Vuitton" or "爱马仕" (Hermès), it often precedes increased store visits and sales.
For LVMH Stock Analysis: Rising search trends in China (especially for LV, Dior) may indicate strengthening demand before it shows up in quarterly earnings. Declining trends may signal weakening interest. Combine with other indicators like WeChat Index and NPS for stronger signals.
Important Caveats:
  • Ultra-luxury buyers (Hermès Birkin, high jewelry) often don't "search" - they buy directly. Low search volume doesn't always mean low sales.
  • Spikes can be driven by news, controversies, or price changes - not just purchase intent. Always investigate sudden changes.
  • Google has only ~3% market share in China, so this captures primarily VPN users and high-income consumers (actually a relevant demographic for luxury).
  • This is one data point among many. Use alongside WeChat Index, NPS scores, and official earnings reports.
Signal Strength Guide
  • ✓ Strong Buy Signal: Both China AND Global trends rising (coordinated global demand).
  • ⚡ China-Specific Strength: China rising while Global flat/down (China market outperforming).
  • ⚠ Mixed Signal: One market up, one down (regional divergence - investigate why).
  • ✗ Weak Signal: Both markets declining (broad softness in consumer interest).
